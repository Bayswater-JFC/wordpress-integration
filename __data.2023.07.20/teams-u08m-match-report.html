Dear Parents and Supporters,

Bayswater Junior Football Team Under 8s kicked off their first game of the season with an impressive display of teamwork, determination and skill. Round 7

Bayswater JFC under 8's had an eventful Round 7 match against Vermont. It was the first game in a while where we faced a team with fewer players, and it was great to witness our players stepping up and volunteering to play a quarter for Vermont. Their display of sportsmanship was commendable, and it was fantastic to see them enjoying the friendly competition against their own teammates.

The focus for today's game was to encourage our players to attack the contest and position themselves at the back of the contest to receive the handball, thus avoiding a crowded forward line. The team responded exceptionally well to this instruction, and it was evident that the hard work put in during training was paying off. Patrick was the first to demonstrate this during the game by calling for the ball out the back, showcasing the team's understanding of the game plan.

Cohen fought hard to win the ball and showed great determination as he finished with a goal, leaving a huge smile on his face. Lewis, displaying admirable sportsmanship, played twice for Vermont and exhibited immense courage by returning to the field after taking a ball to the face, resulting in a blood-stained jumper for his parents to wash.

Max embraced the team plan with enthusiasm, consistently attacking the ball throughout the game. Hudson demonstrated excellent handball skills, executing some impressive link-ups with his teammates.

Flynn and Madden showed great responsiveness to the training work, positioning themselves at the back of play to receive the ball by hand. Their understanding and execution of this tactic were evident during the match, reflecting their commitment to improving their skills.

Overall, it was a memorable game filled with sportsmanship, teamwork, and skill development. The players should be proud of their performance and the way they embraced the game plan. Their enthusiasm and willingness to support one another were key factors in their success.

Congratulations to all the players for their efforts, and let's carry this positive momentum forward to the next challenge.

Round 10

Bayswater JFC under 8's had an exciting match in Round 10 against the Rowville Hawks. After a week's rest, it was great to be back on the field and resume the action.

Over the past few weeks, our focus has been on finding a target out of the middle or at least landing the ball forward of the goals, allowing our forwards to contest the footy. This strategy has proven to be highly effective, as it brings more players into the game and sets us up for future success. Developing the ability to hit targets in our forward line will be crucial in the coming years.

The team displayed excellent teamwork across both the field, with remarkable and effective handballing being a standout feature of our play. Players demonstrated great vision and decision-making, getting to the back of play to receive a handball creating scoring opportunities for the team.

Declan, Johnny, and Hudson showcased their skills by scoring some fantastic goals. Great to see the team celebrating with high fives across the ground.

Jack and Jett exhibited great determination attacking the contest and running the ball up field.

Patrick displayed outstanding goalkeeping skills, demonstrating a strong game sense by pushing back and manning the goal square. His efforts resulted in few brilliant saves, denying the opposition from scoring on multiple occasions which he also seemed quite chuffed with.

Looking ahead, we are excited to have everyone back at training on Friday. The progress we have made as a team is evident, and we are eager to continue working together to further develop our skills and teamwork.

Congratulations to all the players for their contributions in the match, looking forward to having lots of fun with the last few games left of the season.
