Round 1

@ East Ringwood 10/4

Our first game of the season was filled with excitement and unknowns. We’ve set some team rules in place for the season and it was good to see the boys learning and embracing them. East Ringwood were a little short, using a few top up players from the U11s, but the boys still needed to stay switched on and focused, one of our rules and aims this year. The first quarter was a tussle with East Ringwood getting on top on the scoreboard by being first on the ball. A switch of Max into the ruck and Marley into the centre proved positive, and better team play saw us up at halftime 5.5 to 4.2. The second half was a better team effort, with all the boys getting in for the ball first and playing good, hard football. We ended up with a good win, and the pleasing thing was our defensive efforts in the second half. A highlight was Logan kicking four goals in his first game back in the team after his courageous battle last year! Great team effort to start the year

Final Scores: Bayswater 9.10. 64 to East Ringwood 4.4. 28

Goal kickers; Logan Raymond 4, Max Peters, Martin Woollard, Hamish Bithell, Owen Spencer, Izaac Tauiliili, 1 each

Best team players: Marley Parlby, Joel Gunn, Max Peters, Logan Raymond

Round 2

V South Belgrave 24/4

Our first home game of the year saw us take on South Belgrave, a team who got the better of us last year. A great start to the game saw the boys take an early lead and with good attack on the ball (led by Hamish) and great tackling, the boys were up at half time 4.3 to 2.0. Some great individual efforts from Aaron gave his teammates plenty of motivation. The second half saw more of the same, with the boys teaming well with handball, and Zac in particular leading the way with great teamwork when you don’t have the ball. Teamwork is a key part of everything we do this year, and it was great to see Zac leading us. Really good teamwork and being first to the ball led us to a really good win against a good team. Congratulations to Tyler W for getting the Anzac medal from South Belgrave for being a great teammate (thanks to Max copping one in the nethers!! :-D )

Final Scores: Bayswater 6.8. 44 to South Belgrave 3.0. 18

Goal kickers: Mason Cullen 2, Max Peters, Tommy Walker, Izaac Tauiliili, and Dusty Moore 1 each

Best team players: Zac Tebby, Sam Davies, Aaron Busse, Mason Cullen, Tommy Walker

Round 3

V Waverley – 1/5

A trip to Waverley for Round 3 and another slow start for our boys. Although some parts of our game were going well (our shepherding was really good) we had a few boys who were chasing kicks rather than holding their positions, and we were caught out a few times. The boys were rushing their disposals a bit as well, which wasn’t helped by our lack of talk. At half time, though, we were leading 3.5. 23 to 1.1 7. The second half saw a much better effort from the boys in the team areas we focus on – talking, shepherding, and defending for your teammates. Some great football from some of our leaders in Tommy, Joel and Max helped get the results up forward, with great play from Landon and Izaac in particular. The boys managed to secure another good win, with the highlight almost being Hamish’s great snap that turned into a pass to Izaac!

Final Scores: Bayswater 9.9. 63 to Waverley 2.1 13

Goal kickers; Tommy Walker, Owen Spencer, Izaac Tauiliili,2 each, Max Peters, Mason Cullen and Jack Pearson 1 each

Best team players: Joel Gunn, Mason Cullen, Jack Pearson, Landon White, Izaac Tauiliili,

 

Round 4

V Heathmont  8/5

Round 3 at home on Mother’s Day saw us against a very good team in Heathmont, and after 3 straight wins it seemed we were a bit complacent. A disappointing first half as we were second to the ball, and Heathmont’s chatter got under the skin of a few of our boys. At half time we were down 9.6 to 2.5. A talk at half time reminded the boys of one of our key team rules – control what we can. The boys remained more calm and played much more like the way we want to this year. We got our hands on the ball first a lot more (particularly in the third quarter) and led by Billy in the ruck, we were really competitive for the rest of the game. Another great game from Joel in defence as well.

Final Scores: Bayswater 4.9. 32 to Heathmont 10.14. 74

Goal kickers: Tommy Walker 2, Joel Gunn, Dusty Moore 1 each

Best team players: Billy Tournier, Joel Gunn, Tommy Walker, Dusty Moore

Round 5

V The Basin  15/5

We ventured to The Basin in Round 5 and a big game for two of our leaders, Jack and Dusty who played their 50th games together. We asked the boys for an improved mental effort this week, particularly for our two milestone mates, and the boys responded really well. The Basin are a very good, physical team, and while they took it up to our boys physically, our mental strength was there to see, which is really pleasing. Being resilient on the footy field when things are tough or not going our way is a key to achieving success, and it’s also a great took for life in general. In this regard, we were so proud of all the boys for the efforts they put in all game. We were getting hands on the ball first, but our disposal and work off the ball let us down a bit. At half time we were down by a point, 3.0 to 3.1. The second half saw a fitter Basin team move away from us a bit, but the boys never gave up in their efforts, and this will stand us in good stead for the rest of the season. Special mention to some big efforts by Josh, Hamish and Mason, and great games from Ashton, Joel and Martin.

Final Scores: Bayswater 3.2. 20 to The Basin 6.8. 44

Goal kickers; Martin Woollard 2, Logan Raymond 1

Best team players: Josh Toohey, Joel Gunn, Mason Cullen, Hamish Bithell, Ashton Eagles

Round 6

@ North Ringwood 22/5

Round 6 and we trekked down to the back of Quambee Reserve at North Ringwood! For family reasons (an uncle visiting from Darwin) we celebrated Ashton’s 50th today (officially 49th) to he led us out on a coolish morning. The boys seemed fairly switched on before the game despite the unusual changeroom situation (none) and were warmed up quite well This resulted in some much improved teamwork from everyone, with lots of running, shepherding, and particularly talking which was very pleasing to us. With Joel working hard in the ruck, Mason’s hard running and Tommy really focused down back, we were leading quite comfortably at half time 5.5 to 0.0. We asked for an improved effort from the boys in the second half (as we do each week) and despite an even third quarter on the scoreboard, they all responded really well. Talking and teamwork was a feature, and despite a little frustration at the end of the third quarter, the boys kept their mental strength and ran out good winners. A super game from Tyler Barbour on the wing most of the day, a great running effort from Sam, and tough efforts all day from Martin, Tommy and Dusty. A great team effort, boys.

Final Scores: Bayswater 11.9. 75 to North Ringwood 1.1. 7

Goal kickers; Max Peters 4, Ashton Eagles and Dusty Moore 2 each, Joel Gunn, Martin Woolard, and Zac Tebby 1 each

Best team players: Tyler Barbour, Sam Davies, Mason Cullen, Tommy Walker, Max Peters, Martin Woolard

Round 7

V Rowville  29/5

Back at home against Rowville in Round 7 and the boys started reasonably well through our teamwork which has been building over the past couple of weeks. An even first quarter saw us kicking rather blindly into the forward line, but a quick quarter time chat and some good feedback from the boys saw an improved second quarter with some great handball chains and bringing teammates into the game. At half time we led 5.6 to 2.0. Funnily enough, the third quarter saw us back to panic kicking into the forward line, but we could see that mentally the boys were switched on and focused. This was rewarded in the last quarter with some great handball and teamwork, and we ran out reasonably comfortable winners. A great defensive effort in the second half to keep Rowville scoreless.  A couple of special mentions – to Owen for his great tenacity in running to the right spots, Tyler Barbour for an outstanding game on the wing.

Final Scores: Bayswater 11.10. 76 to Rowville 2.0. 12

Goal kickers; Tommy Walker 4, Mason Cullen 2, Martin Woolard, Joel Gunn, Tyler Walker, Jack Pearson and Zac Tebby 1 each

Best team players: Tyler Barbour, Tyler Walker, Zac Tebby, Tommy Walker, Joel Gunn

Round 8

V East Ringwood  5/6

Round 8 and another home game in Round 8 taking on East Ringwood again. We had discussed the need to have a good start, and to also focus on the job at hand rather than looking at ladder positions and previous scores. To the boys’ credit they came out firing for our best first quarter of the year. It was great to have Aaron back after a little break and he showed why we love having him in our team with some great attack on the ball. Max was leading the way up forward with some great pressure, and the teamwork was continuing to improve, which as coaches we love. At half time we lead 7.3 to 0.0. The second half was pleasing in that the boys stuck to our plan rather than being influenced by scores, and our continued improved teamwork led to a comfortable win. Another great job from our defence, and Max certainly benefitted up forward from the great team play up the ground all day.

Final Scores: Bayswater 14. 5. 89 to East Ringwood 0.1 1

Goal kickers; Max Peters 7, Joel Gunn, Dusty Moore 2 each, Zac Tebby, Tommy Walker and Owen Spencer 1 each

Best team players:  Ashton Eagles, Martin Woollard, Mason Cullen, Tommy Walker, Izaac Tauiliili

Round 9

V South Belgrave  19/6

A trip to South Belgrave to face the top team was a good test for the boys who have shown improvement over the past few weeks. We again mentioned about the need to focus and control what we can, and this would be really important against this team. The boys started really strongly and kept moving the ball into the forward line well. Our midfield and backline were also playing well, but unfortunately we couldn’t quite find the goals. The middle two quarters proved the difference for us, as we allowed South Belgrave to intimidate us a bit, and the boys were fumbling and unsure with their skills, which isn’t how we’ve been playing recently. However, a concerted effort after some ¾ time fun saw the boys have a great last quarter, the highlight being Billy’s goal in his first game back! A tough loss against a tough time, but hopefully the boys can learn and grow. A super game from Tommy in the ruck, and of course Dusty in the middle.

Final Scores: Bayswater 2.6. 18 to South Belgrave 6.8. 44

Goal kickers; Logan Raymond, Billy Tournier

Best team players: Tommy Walker, Dusty Moore, Billy Tournier, Izaac Tauiliili
