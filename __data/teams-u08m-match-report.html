Round 1

Vs Wantirna south white
10am - Lewis oval.

Well the weather couldn't of been any better for our under 8's first game of the year. Sun was shining, a little anxiety from the boys of what was to come, combned with excitment. 

The game plan was simple: Get the ball and run towards our goals, no more than 10 meters (we measured out 10 meters before the game, so we all could see what was 10 meters was) and kick or handball as far as possible to your team mate.  Lastley have as much fun as possible. We certainly had lots of fun.

Special call out to Tommy Delbridge who unfortuanly was not able to play due to an arm being in plaster. Not to worry, Tommy joined the officals and helped pick this weeks captain and vice captain, hand our half time lolly snake and share some easter eggs at the end of the game. Thank you Tommy for attending and supporting.

I was really proud of all the boys who put thier hand up to turn theier gurnsey inside out and play for the Wantirna team. This allowed us to have no interchange and everyone player play a full game.

We ended with cheers for the umpire and our first go at the team song. Well done lads.

Thank you to all the families that came to support the first game and all the work put in to get us to that point.

I cant wait for our next game together. Well done U8 baysy

Round 7

What a pleasant day it was Sunday considering how wet it is now (monday) and this week. The sun was shinning and a tad crisp. Ideal U8 playing conditions. The boys were bouncing off the walls as per usual ready to play. It brings a smile to everyone to see them so excited to play each Sunday.
We were very fortunate to have just about the full team line up of 16 on Sunday. Very exciting.
 
The team continued working on our 3 simple strategies.:
  1. Be first to the ball
  2. Run as hard as you can towards our goals when you get the ball.
  3. Kick or handball as long as you can towards goals, space and team mates etc......
Simple but effective.
