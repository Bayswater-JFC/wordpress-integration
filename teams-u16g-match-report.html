Round 1

Today we played our first game of the season. It rained for a fair part of the day but lucky for us, we got no rain for the game.

We started the game really well. Our more experienced girls took charge and controlled the game. We had a very good lead at quarter time.

After the first quarter and with a big lead, we mixed things up and moved everyone around and gave some of our less experienced players more opportunities to play on the ball. We continued to dominate the game with all players having a go and making the most of it when the ball came their way. Nice to see some of our quieter girls coming out of their shells.

We have definitely improved since our practice match which was very encouraging to see.

Round 2

Today we played Boronia at their senior ground which is a big ground.

We had a few players out so we only had 16 players against 20 for Boronia so we played with no bench.

We knew it was going to be a tough game with Boronia winning the flag last year and them having girls resting on the bench.

We went in with a very positive attitude and to try and play the best we can and making sure we talked and helped each other.

We started really well. It was pretty close in the first quarter but we worked a little bit harder to get a good lead at quarter time.

The second quarter was very even again but as we got tired, Boronia were able to kick a couple of goals to even things up at half time.

The second half was challenging for us and you could see the girls getting tired as the game went on, having no bench to rest the players.

Boronia probably had a bit more of the ball than us but our backline stood strong and did an amazing job to stop them kicking goals.

It was a very close finish but we ended up winning by 3 points even though the other team had more scoring shots.

The game could’ve gone either way and luckily for us, it went our way.

Goals. Haylee, Matilda, Shaylee

Best. Matilda, Shaylee, Haylee, Milly, Chloe, Ella, Saanvi, Tiarnah.

Round 8

Today was a big game for our team. We were playing Waverley Blues at home and we had heard they were a pretty quick team so we needed to be on the ball.

We also had our captain Matilda playing her 100th game so that was exciting for the playing group but gave us a little more reason to play well and hopefully get the win.

The game started how most games do. Both teams were full of energy and showed a lot of eagerness to get the ball. It was pretty even for most of the quarter but there were times we had a bit more of the ball which helped us get an early lead at quarter time. The second quarter was pretty similar but it seemed like there was a scoring end and Waverly was kicking to that end so they caught back our lead to be even at half time.

The third quarter was very even again but we had the scoring end so we were able to get a 2 goal lead by three quarter time.

We needed to dig deep and the last quarter to try and hold onto our lead. We didn’t start the last quarter very well at all. We seemed to lose our concentration for a bit so we weren’t picking up our players or attacking the ball how we should so Waverley had a lot of the ball for the first part of the last quarter. A little lucky for us that they missed a couple of shots for goal so we stayed in front. Once we switched back on, we got back to doing what we needed to do and managed to kick a couple of goals making sure we won the game. We won 47-33.

We had a lot of girls step up today which really made a difference for our team.

One of our best team efforts this year so far.

Round 10

With the other sides all improving, we need to improve as well to stay competitive.

Hopefully we can bounce back next week.
